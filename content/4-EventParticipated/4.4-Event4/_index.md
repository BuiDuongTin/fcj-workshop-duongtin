# "AWS Study Tour - Swinburne Vietnam x AWS Vietnam" Event Summary

## Event Objectives

This study tour was organized to give Swinburne Vietnam students a more practical view of cloud computing and AWS in real business environments. Instead of learning only from theory, participants could hear directly from people working in the industry and understand how cloud skills are used in hiring, engineering work, data engineering, communication, and career development.

The event also aimed to help students see the gap between classroom learning and enterprise expectations. Through the sharing sessions, participants learned that technical knowledge alone is not enough; attitude, teamwork, visibility in the community, public speaking, and continuous self-development are also important for a long-term career in cloud and technology.

## General Information

- **Event name:** AWS Study Tour - Swinburne Vietnam x AWS Vietnam
- **Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, July 4, 2026
- **Location:** Study tour session with AWS Vietnam and Swinburne Vietnam speakers
- **Format:** Career talk, technical sharing, and community learning session
- **Role:** Participant
- **Main topics:** Cloud careers, AWS market trends, data engineering, communication skills, AI usage, networking, and professional growth

## Speakers and Main Topics

The program included several speakers who shared different perspectives on cloud and technology careers.

### List of Speakers

- **Quynh Mai** - Opening and event introduction
- **Nguyen Tran Minh Duy** - Industry Learning Officer, Swinburne Vietnam
- **Nguyen Gia Hung** - Head of Solution Architect, AWS Vietnam, and founder of AWS First Cloud AI Journey
- **Banh Cam Vinh** - Data Engineer, Renova Cloud
- **Nhu Tran** - Account Manager, AWS Vietnam
- **Khang Nguyen** - Solution Architect, Cloud Kinesis

## Key Sessions

### Study Tour Introduction and Community Support

The event began with an introduction from Quynh Mai, who presented the study tour as a special learning opportunity for Swinburne Vietnam students, including students from Can Tho. The session highlighted that this was not just a normal visit, but a chance to see real cloud practices and hear directly from professionals working in the field.

Nguyen Tran Minh Duy shared how Swinburne Vietnam has continuously brought students to AWS-related learning activities for three consecutive years, from 2024 to 2026. He also emphasized the importance of the collaboration between the university and the AWS First Cloud AI Journey community, which helps students learn from real experience and continue growing through community support.

### Cloud Career Market and AWS Industry Outlook

Nguyen Gia Hung opened the first major sharing session by discussing the current cloud job market and the expectations placed on young talent. One of the strongest messages was that companies today do not hire purely based on attitude or certificates. They expect real technical understanding, practical experience, and the ability to work with important cloud concepts.

He pointed out that the cloud market in Vietnam has grown quickly, with AWS revenue increasing dramatically over the past several years while traditional hardware-based business models have declined. This reflects a broader shift toward cloud-first strategies in modern enterprises.

The session also explained that AWS continues to invest in Vietnam through three main areas: local talent, physical infrastructure, and support for export-oriented businesses. For students, the main takeaway was clear: cloud skills are not optional anymore, and the market increasingly rewards people who can build real solutions and show evidence of their work.

### Data Engineering: School vs Enterprise Reality

In the next session, Banh Cam Vinh talked about the difference between what students learn in school and what data engineers actually face in companies. He explained that university projects often use clean, small, and well-defined datasets, while real business data is messy, incomplete, changing, and connected to multiple teams.

The session showed that enterprise work is much more demanding. Deadlines are tighter, requirements change faster, and the impact of mistakes is far more serious because it can affect business operations and revenue. For that reason, data engineers need not only technical knowledge, but also flexibility, ownership, communication skills, and the ability to work with many departments.

He also encouraged students to master the fundamentals, understand the real workflow, and build personal projects that go beyond tutorials. In the end, strong fundamentals and practical problem-solving matter more than simply following instructions.

### Communication, Visibility, and Career Growth

Nhu Tran's session focused on an often overlooked but critical part of career development: communication. She spoke about fear of making mistakes, fear of being judged, and the hesitation many students feel when speaking in public or approaching managers.

The session encouraged participants to practice public speaking, small talk, and relationship building. A key idea was that career opportunities often come through people who know your work, trust your attitude, and remember your presence in the community. Visibility matters, and networking is not superficial when it is built on genuine learning and contribution.

This part of the event also emphasized resilience. Nhu Tran shared that success often comes after many rejections, and that persistence, not just talent, is what creates long-term growth.

### Learning With AI While Keeping the Fundamentals Strong

Khang Nguyen, a former Swinburne student and current Solution Architect at Cloud Kinesis, closed the event by talking about learning in the age of AI. He reminded students that AI is a powerful tool, but it should not replace real understanding.

The main message was to ask "why" more often, learn from mistakes, and keep building a strong technical foundation. He also described the balance between passion, responsibility, and practical benefits such as salary, relationships, and knowledge. His message reinforced that attitude is one of the first things employers notice.

This session tied the entire event together by showing that modern cloud professionals need both technical depth and the ability to learn continuously as technology changes.

## Key Takeaways

The study tour highlighted several lessons that are important for students and early-career engineers.

First, cloud careers are becoming more competitive, and employers expect practical ability rather than only academic knowledge or certificates.

Second, the gap between school and enterprise work is real. Students need to understand data quality, changing requirements, collaboration, and the consequences of production issues.

Third, communication and networking are career skills, not just soft extras. Being visible in a community, speaking clearly, and building relationships can create real opportunities.

Fourth, AI should be used as a tool to support learning and productivity, but not as a substitute for critical thinking or technical fundamentals.

## Applying to Study and Work

The biggest practical lesson from this event is that career growth in cloud and data requires more than passing classes. Students should build portfolios, publish projects, practice communication, and join communities where real industry conversations happen.

For cloud learning, it is important to understand core concepts such as Kubernetes, architecture, networking, security, observability, and deployment patterns. For data engineering, students should practice handling dirty data, changing requirements, and end-to-end workflows rather than only working with ideal datasets.

For career development, students can apply the event's advice by improving public speaking, building a stronger online presence, asking better questions, and staying consistent over time. These habits help create trust and make future opportunities easier to access.

## Personal Reflection

This study tour was valuable because it connected classroom learning with real industry expectations. It made the cloud career path feel more concrete and showed that success in technology depends on both knowledge and behavior.

The most memorable lessons were the importance of community, the need to keep learning, and the fact that attitude often matters as much as technical skill. I also realized that AI is changing the way people work, but it does not replace the need to understand fundamentals deeply.

After the event, I felt more motivated to improve my cloud knowledge, strengthen my communication skills, and build projects that show real ability instead of only classroom results.

## Conclusion

Overall, the AWS study tour with Swinburne Vietnam and AWS Vietnam was a meaningful learning experience that helped students better understand cloud careers, enterprise data work, communication, and the role of AI in modern learning.

The biggest lesson from the event is that technology careers are built on a combination of technical skill, clear communication, perseverance, and continuous self-development. Cloud and AI are important, but the people who grow the most are the ones who keep learning, keep building, and keep showing up in the community.
